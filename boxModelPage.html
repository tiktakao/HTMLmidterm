The Box Model | {padding/border/margin}

The Box Model is a series of boxes surrounding your content. Each of these can be manipulated separately and have distinct characteristics.

  • Directly surrounding but not including your content is the padding. The padding inherits the background color of the content and creates space between your content and the border.
  • The border has a variety of styles to choose from, including solid, dotted, dashed, groove, ridge, and more.
  • Outside the border is the margin. The margin is colorless and cannot be styled. Its sole purpose is to create space between the content it contains, and the border of its neighboring containers to prevent collision or overlap. Make special note that a margin keeps a neighboring border at bay, but will overlap with a neighboring margin. This means that the distance between two containers is the size of the largest margin between the two containers.

If you would like to inspect the padding, border, or margin of an element online, you can right-click and 'inspect.'

CSS Input:


.exampleBoxModel{
    padding: 10px;
    border: 15px solid;
    background-color: #FF6666; <!--red-->
    border-color: #6699FF; <!--blue-->
}

Because it is not possible to directly change the color of a margin, I have underlaid a second container colored yellow to simulate a margin of 25px.
